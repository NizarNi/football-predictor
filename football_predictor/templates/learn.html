Analytics Glossary

Expected Goals (xG)

Definition: A statistical measure that quantifies the quality of a shot by calculating the probability it will result in a goal, based on factors like distance, angle, body part used, and defensive pressure.

Example: A shot from 6 yards out in the center of the goal might have an xG of 0.8 (80% chance of scoring), while a long-range effort from 30 yards might only have an xG of 0.05 (5% chance).

Why it matters: xG helps identify teams that create high-quality chances vs. those that rely on luck. A team consistently outperforming their xG may be due for regression.

Elo Ratings

Definition: A rating system originally developed for chess, adapted for football to measure team strength based on historical match results. Higher-rated teams are expected to beat lower-rated teams.

Example: Manchester City (Elo: 1963) vs. Burnley (Elo: 1713) → City expected to win ~75% of the time based on the 250-point rating difference.

Why it matters: Elo provides an objective measure of team strength independent of current form or injuries. Our platform uses ClubElo.com data (updated twice daily) to calculate win probabilities.

PPDA (Passes Per Defensive Action)

Definition: A metric measuring defensive pressing intensity. It's calculated as: (Opponent passes allowed) ÷ (Defensive actions by team). Lower PPDA = more aggressive pressing.

Interpretation:
• <8 PPDA: Extreme high press (Liverpool, Man City style)
• 8-12: High press
• 12-15: Medium press
• 15-20: Low press
• >20: Passive/deep defensive block

Why it matters: Understanding a team's pressing style helps predict match dynamics and betting outcomes (e.g., high-pressing teams create more turnovers and chances).

Arbitrage Betting

Definition: A risk-free betting strategy that exploits odds differences across multiple bookmakers to guarantee profit regardless of the match outcome.

Example: If Bookmaker A offers Home Win @ 2.10, Draw @ 3.50, Away Win @ 4.00, and Bookmaker B offers different odds, you might find a combination where betting on all outcomes guarantees a 2-5% profit.

How to use it: Our platform automatically detects arbitrage opportunities and shows optimal stake distribution. These are rare but highly profitable when found.

Value Bets

Definition: A bet where your calculated probability of an outcome is higher than the bookmaker's implied probability, indicating potential long-term profit.

Golden Rule: Only bet when your model shows higher probability than the market.
Example: Elo model predicts 72% home win, but market odds imply 58% → 14% edge = VALUE BET ✅

Why it matters: Value betting is the foundation of profitable sports betting. Our Hybrid Model (60% Elo + 40% Market) helps identify these opportunities by comparing objective ratings with market sentiment.

Beginner's Guide to Our Predictions

Step 1: How to Read Our Predictions

  • Market Odds: Consensus from 30+ bookmakers. Shows what the betting market believes.
  • Elo Model: Objective probability based on historical team strength (updated twice daily from ClubElo.com).
  • Hybrid Model: Our recommended prediction (60% Elo + 40% Market) balancing historical performance with current sentiment.
  • Confidence Score: Higher confidence (≥75%) indicates stronger agreement between models.

Step 2: Understanding Probabilities

Implied Probability = 1 ÷ Decimal Odds

Examples:
• Odds of 2.00 → 50% probability (1 ÷ 2.00 = 0.50)
• Odds of 3.00 → 33.3% probability
• Odds of 1.50 → 66.7% probability

Safe Bet Filters:

  • ≥88% confidence: Extremely safe bets (1 in 8 loss rate)
  • ≥75% confidence: Very safe bets (1 in 4 loss rate)
  • ≥60% confidence: Safe bets (2 in 5 loss rate)

Step 3: When to Bet vs. Not Bet

✅ Good Betting Opportunities:

  • Arbitrage opportunities (risk-free profit)
  • Value bets where Elo > Market by ≥10%
  • High confidence predictions (≥75%) with good odds
  • Matches with comprehensive data (xG, Elo, form all available)

❌ Avoid Betting When:

  • Low confidence (<55%) - too uncertain
  • Missing critical data (no xG or Elo ratings)
  • Emotional attachment to a team (bias clouds judgment)
  • Chasing losses (never bet more to recover losses)

Step 4: Bankroll Management Basics

The Kelly Criterion (recommended):

Formula: Stake % = (Probability × Odds - 1) ÷ (Odds - 1)
Example: 60% win probability at 2.00 odds → (0.60 × 2.00 - 1) ÷ (2.00 - 1) = 20% of bankroll

Flat Staking (safer for beginners):

  • Bet 1-3% of bankroll per wager
  • Safest bets: 3% stake
  • Medium confidence: 2% stake
  • Value bets with risk: 1% stake

Warning: Never bet more than 5% of your bankroll on a single match. Proper bankroll management is the difference between long-term profit and going broke.
